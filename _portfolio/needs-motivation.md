## Overview
My work in this area develops and tests a theory of human needs and motivation, with the goal of explaining—and ultimately predicting—why people behave the way they do across educational, organizational, and everyday contexts. This theory helps explain not only whether people use their abilities, but also what ends they pursue—connecting to my work on wisdom.

## Core idea
We propose that specific psychological needs and motivational processes drive patterns of behavior and development. Current work focuses on empirically testing the theory’s assumptions and predictions and examining how these needs influence real-world outcomes.

## Current and planned projects
- **Theory paper (under review):** A proposed theory of human needs and motivation.
- **Empirical tests (in progress):** Studies designed to evaluate key predictions of the theory.
- **Forthcoming work:** Examining how proposed needs influence behavior and decision-making, including implications for educational and policy contexts.

## Why it matters
Understanding needs and motivation can help educators support students’ optimal development and inform policy approaches aimed at changing behavior and improving well-being. 
This research also provides a unifying lens for my other work on how people use their abilities and how leaders create positive change.

This line of work builds on my broader agenda: understanding how needs and motivations shape the deployment of ability toward positive outcomes.
## Overview
This research program asks: **How can leaders create positive change in the world?** I study wisdom, wise decision-making, and leadership styles that support ethical, constructive, and sustainable outcomes.

## Key directions
- **Theory development:** Work proposing a framework for wisdom and wise leadership (including transformational approaches).
- **Empirical studies:** Projects identifying different wise leadership styles and examining the characteristics associated with wisdom and wise action.
- **Person–task–situation perspective:** I emphasize that wisdom and leadership depend on contextual demands. I have also contributed a book chapter discussing how situation and task shape wisdom-related behavior.

## Why it matters
Understanding wise leadership can inform education, organizational practice, and policy by clarifying the conditions under which leaders make decisions that benefit both individuals and society.
